Summer in Flensburg

Quick facts

Flensburg is a town at the tip of the Flensburg Fjord in northern Germany. Flensburg is an independent city in the north of Schleswig-Holstein. After Kiel and Lübeck, Flensburg is the third largest city in the state with around 90,000 inhabitants, the largest in the Schleswig region and the northernmost urban district in Germany . Its brick gabled Nordertor, built around 1595, is the last remaining city gate. The Flensburger Schifffahrtsmuseum chronicles the town's seafaring past. Nearby, the shipyard museum Museumswerft displays recreated historic ships and hosts boatbuilding classes. Museumsberg Flensburg explores art and cultural history from the Middle Ages onward. Flensburg is the center of the Danish minority in southern Schleswig , Flensburg took on a pioneering role for the recognition of national minorities after the referendum in Schleswig in 1920 and the Bonn-Copenhagen declarations of 1955.

The Norderstraße (Low German : Norderstrat )is a historic thoroughfare in the old town of Flensburg. The largely cobbled street is considered a multicultural "trendy district of Flensburg", in which, in addition to the Danish minority, alternative left-wing subculture and a number of restaurants with international cuisine, scene shops and artist studios have also settled. Because of their shoefitior hanging shoes, the New York travel magazine Travel + Leisure named Norderstrasse one of the 18 “craziest streets in the world.”
